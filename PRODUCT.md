# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo and early-stage SaaS founders, pre-product-market-fit, trying to get their first real customers without cold-pitching, paid ads, or a sales team. Time-constrained, wearing every hat, and specifically anxious about *distribution* rather than building — the exact voice already surfaced by the app's own Positioning feature: "I can build the product but marketing is not my thing," "I don't even know where my users hang out online," "just trying to get to my first 10 paying customers." Their job to be done on any given screen is either "find the real people already expressing this pain point right now" or "act on one of them without sounding like an ad."

## Product Purpose

Getrive listens across channels (Reddit and Hacker News today) for people already expressing, in their own words, the exact pain point a founder's product solves — scores relevance with AI, then helps the founder respond authentically instead of cold-pitching. It never posts, DMs, tweets, or emails on the founder's behalf anywhere: every output is a draft the founder reviews, edits, copies, and sends themselves. Attribution tracks real signups back to the reply/channel that produced them, so "did this actually get me users" has a real answer, not a vanity metric. Success looks like a founder going from zero distribution channel to their first real customers using only genuine, one-to-one conversations Getrive surfaced.

## Brand Personality

Restrained signal-ops / technical. Precision and quiet confidence over friendliness or hype — three words: **vigilant, precise, indie-technical**. The existing UI already commits to this: "listening node," "phase," "calibration" framing; monospace uppercase micro-labels; phase-tracker sidebars; a topographic contour texture; a dark near-black deep-teal palette. This is deliberate brand DNA (built via a prior AIDesigner pass, not a default) and carries forward into every new surface.

## Anti-references

- The generic SaaS-cream AI-default dashboard: warm off-white/sand backgrounds, identical card grids, gradient hero-metric stat blocks. Avoid categorically — this product already has real visual identity and reverting to that template would be a regression, not a refresh.
- Playful/cutesy startup tone: no mascots, bouncy illustrations, or overly casual copy. This is a tool for a founder under real distribution pressure, not a consumer toy.
- Corporate enterprise navy-and-gray blandness. Keep the indie/technical edge; this is not a stodgy enterprise B2B dashboard.

## Design Principles

1. **"Nothing posted without you."** Every AI output is a draft the founder reviews and sends themselves. No surface should ever imply automated outbound action — this is a load-bearing trust promise, already stated verbatim in the product (onboarding sidebar footer, Settings' Reddit-connection copy).
2. **Precision over decoration.** The signal-ops framing (phase trackers, mono-uppercase labels, topographic texture, glowing accent dots) is restrained, not maximalist. Motifs support clarity and support a "this is a serious instrument" feeling; they never perform technicality for its own sake.
3. **Show real signal, not vanity metrics.** Surfaces should foreground genuine, specific evidence — an actual quoted pain point, a real audience phrase, a real subreddit/source — over generic dashboard stats or gradient hero numbers.
4. **Respect founder anxiety.** The user is stressed about distribution at day zero. Copy, empty states, and error states should read as a calm, capable ally — never add friction, judgment, or hype.
5. **Extend the existing system, don't fork it.** New surfaces (Sources, Outreach, This Week) must read as the same product: reuse the established dark brand kit, component patterns, and interaction rhythms rather than introducing a new visual language per feature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA care: sufficient color contrast on the dark palette (worth explicit verification given the muted sage accent and off-white-on-near-black combination), full keyboard navigation through forms and the onboarding wizard, and reduced-motion alternatives for existing animations (pulsing dots, scan-line effects, staggered card reveals). No unusual or stricter requirements beyond solid baseline practice at this stage.
